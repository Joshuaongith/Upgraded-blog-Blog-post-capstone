My name is Joshua Uzochukwu, a recent Computer Engineering graduate (B.Eng, November 2025) from All Nations University, with a deep commitment to continuous technical growth. Throughout my learning journey so far, I have acquired hands-on skills in backend software development, space systems technology, hardware configuration, and audio-visual production.

In this current chapter of my career, I am focused on sharpening my skills in Python programming and the rapidly expanding field of artificial intelligence and automation. I recently secured a scholarship for the AI and Automation course (Hajime cohort) at TS Academy. Simultaneously, I am advancing through Dr. Angela Yu's "100 Days of Code" Python Pro Bootcamp. So far, my progression through the curriculum has covered foundational scripting, object-oriented programming, data scraping, web development, and building comprehensive backend systems, including fully functional RESTful APIs using Flask and SQLAlchemy. To consistently reinforce my algorithmic problem-solving abilities alongside project development, I am also actively working through the HackerRank 30 Days of Code challenge.

My drive for tackling complex, real-world problems is deeply rooted in my university experience. During my time as a Student Coordinator and STEM Educator at the All Nations University Space Systems Technology Laboratory (ANU-SSTL), I contributed significantly to the African Space Station Project and the STEM for Underserved Communities (STEM4U) initiative. STEM4U is a community outreach project focused on bridging the digital divide by introducing modern technologies to indigenous and unreached populations. For these outreaches, I provided operational support and taught students fundamental STEM concepts, such as amateur radio, telemetry, and signal processing, through hands-on practicals. My work in the lab also involved direct hardware and telemetry experience, including assisting with satellite monitoring, signal analysis, and deploying environmental monitoring tools like the Clarity device and Aeronet photometer.

Subsequently, I represented All Nations University at two major industry events: the Ghana Space Conference in October 2025 and the 5th Arab and Africa Impact Cratering and Astrogeology Conference (AICAC V) in November 2025, where I had the privilege of sharing updates and presenting the roadmap for the African Space Station Project.

In addition to my engineering studies, I actively served in the ANU Media and ANFGC Main Campus Media teams. Managing signal flow, digital mixing consoles, and visual presentation software allowed me to build a strong, practical foundation in live audio engineering, digital streaming, photography, and video editing.

I am enthusiastic about exploring new opportunities to apply my diverse, multidisciplinary engineering background in solving complex technical challenges.
